Why Maui?

Why did we choose to get married on Maui when there are so many places that are not Maui to choose from? When we both have lived in Oregon so long? Well, Justin is from Maui, and Abi and Justin have made many memories there. The wedding venue is actually Seabury Hall, Justin's old high school. Seabury is not only a beautiful place and fantastic wedding venue, but also a place of memory and history. A number of childhood friends and family connections still live on the island, and our closest friends and most immediate family members approved the idea of a celebration on Maui about seven years ago, when we first came up with the idea. For us, this "destination wedding" is an opportunity to share a beautiful and special place with those who are able to join us...

FAQ

Is Kahului (OGG) the right airport?

Yes! That’s the main Maui airport and the airport you want to fly into.

Where should we stay?

We don’t have an easy answer to this, because there are so many options, variables for what you might want, and good deals. There are folks staying at Airbnb locations, folks renting houses, and folks getting package deals with airfare, hotel, and rental car. You can stay down country (warmer and closer to the beach) or up country (cooler and closer to the wedding). Lahaina might be a little far (Google estimates it would take 50 minutes to an hour and 20 minutes to get to Seabury from Lahaina on our wedding day), but it would still be workable if you wanted to stay there.

What kinds of things should we do?

Well, Maui has excellent hiking, snorkeling, and similar activities. A trip to the Seven Sacred Pools on the Hana side of the island would be an intense all day endeavour, but absolutely worth it unless you have a phobia about winding, narrow roads. :) A trip up to the Haleakala crater is definitely worth it, just don’t wear your bathing suit -- it gets much colder up at that higher elevation. Certain beaches have particularly great snorkeling, and others have other stand out characteristics.

How should I dress for the wedding?

For the wedding, we'd like a casual but not too casual dress code. (No jeans, please! Collared shirts, which can include Hawaiian shirts, or a nice blouse. Khaki shorts would be fine.)

If you want to dress up, it's fine to do so, as well. :) The bride will be wearing a traditional wedding dress, so you shouldn't worry about dressing up more than she does :) It's an outdoor wedding on grass, so please plan accordingly. :)
