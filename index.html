Justin and Abi's Suggestions for What to Do on Maui

Helicopter Tour
or airplane tour (The helicopter tour is more expensive, but it allows you to go to waterfalls that can ONLY be reached that way!)
Go Snorkeling

There are many great options for snorkeling, but if you aren't comfortable swimming or you want to visit a prime snorkeling location only reachable by boat, take a Molikini snorkel tour. If you’re wanting to take children there, The Four Winds or some Pacific Whale Foundation boats are your best bet.

If you want an opportunity to swim with dolphins, go snorkeling on Lanai!

The Lahaina area is great for snorkeling, too. Justin’s dad’s absolute favorite is Napili Bay. It’s a bit in the middle of nowhere, but it’s the prettiest and has a high chance of turtles! (No touching!) For easier access on Lahaina side, go to Airport Beach.

Two other great options closer to Wailea are Makena Surf (between Wailea and Makena) and Ulua Beach in Wailea. Justin and I will be snorkeling right by our hotel in Wailea, too, because there’s great snorkeling there. Many good options!

Lahaina

Snorkeling is great in Lahaina, and you might see a sea turtle snorkeling or even dining (just never ever TOUCH a sea turtle! Seriously! As Justin says, suggest someone you dislike touch one, so you can watch them go to jail.)

We loved eating at Honu restaurant, favorite of renowned baker Harold Hardcastle.

Old Lahaina Luau for a luau experience.

Ziplining!

What a fun way to enjoy the natural beauty of Maui. Apparently, there are as many options for how to do this as there are types of people and adventurers! :)

Our friend Seth’s favorite, which we think we’ll try, is the Piiholo Ranch Adventures tree top adventure, where you are up on platforms in the trees (and clipped in) the whole time.

If you want to go FAST and huge, do The Flying Hawaiian for some of the biggest and fastest ziplining in the whole state! (Abi will not be doing this one; someone has died on this zipline, and that’s not a sign of awesomeness to her. She doesn’t even like rollercoasters. ;))

Haleakala Crater

Get a reservation to go up to the crater at sunrise (This is the only way to see the sunrise on Maui! Bring very warm clothes; expect freezing temperatures.)

Hike down into the caldera

Go at another time (Sunset is also spectacular, but it's less reliable as there could be clouds obstructing your view.)

Be one of those people who takes one of the biking tour buses up and then bikes down from the crater (Justin hates how these people take over the roads, but accepts that it is a fun way to enjoy Maui. ;)

Fishing & Whale Watching Tours
Hiking
Maui is a great place to hike! The best spots would be recommended by a local who lives on Maui, but you can easily find many great options

So, this is a difficult and unpleasant journey. However, Hana-side is beautiful, lush, and memorable. Justin and Abi took a memorable trip together once, and they thoroughly enjoyed the experience. Why Maui?

Why did we choose to get married on Maui when there are so many places that are not Maui to choose from? When we both have lived in Oregon so long? Well, Justin is from Maui, and Abi and Justin have made many memories there. The wedding venue is actually Seabury Hall, Justin's old high school. Seabury is not only a beautiful place and fantastic wedding venue, but also a place of memory and history. A number of childhood friends and family connections still live on the island, and our closest friends and most immediate family members approved the idea of a celebration on Maui about seven years ago, when we first came up with the idea. For us, this "destination wedding" is an opportunity to share a beautiful and special place with those who are able to join us...

FAQ

Is Kahului (OGG) the right airport?

Yes! That’s the main Maui airport and the airport you want to fly into.

Where should we stay?

We don’t have an easy answer to this, because there are so many options, variables for what you might want, and good deals. There are folks staying at Airbnb locations, folks renting houses, and folks getting package deals with airfare, hotel, and rental car. You can stay down country (warmer and closer to the beach) or up country (cooler and closer to the wedding). Lahaina might be a little far (Google estimates it would take 50 minutes to an hour and 20 minutes to get to Seabury from Lahaina on our wedding day), but it would still be workable if you wanted to stay there.

How should I dress for the wedding?

For the wedding, we'd like a casual but not too casual dress code. (No jeans, please! Collared shirts, which can include Hawaiian shirts, or a nice blouse. Khaki shorts would be fine.)

If you want to dress up, it's fine to do so, as well. :) The bride will be wearing a traditional wedding dress, so you shouldn't worry about dressing up more than she does :) It's an outdoor wedding on grass, so please plan accordingly. :)
